## Top Stories

### Foundry Services Launches Zcash Mining Pool in April

The biggest institutional signal of the week: [Foundry Services](https://x.com/FoundryServices), the largest Bitcoin mining pool (31% of BTC hashrate), announced it will launch a Zcash mining pool in April. This is the first institutional-grade mining pool dedicated to ZEC. The news coincides with ZEC hitting an [all-time high hashrate](https://x.com/FoundryServices/status/2034343090228498657). As one analyst [noted](https://x.com/i/status/2035027918137520344): "Record network security + muted sentiment = textbook accumulation zone."

### Zodl CrossPay Goes Live via NEAR Intents

Zodl wallet shipped CrossPay, enabling [shielded cross-chain payments](https://x.com/i/status/2033931096166174902). Send ZEC privately, recipient gets BTC, stablecoins, or any supported asset — powered by NEAR Intents. Users are already [using it daily](https://x.com/i/status/2033944961067192521). This is a major UX milestone: Zcash privacy extending beyond the Zcash network for the first time in a consumer wallet.

### Delphi Digital: 30% of ZEC Now Shielded

Research firm Delphi Digital [published analysis](https://x.com/Delphi_Digital/status/2035098447817175061) showing ~30% of all circulating ZEC is now held in shielded pools — growth continuing despite tough market conditions. ## Protocol & Development

### Zebra 4.2.0 Released

The Zcash Foundation shipped [Zebra 4.2.0](https://github.com/ZcashFoundation/zebra/releases/tag/v4.2.0) with significant improvements:

- `gettxout` RPC — enables wallet devs to query unspent transparent outputs directly
- OpenRPC spec — replaces legacy OpenAPI docs, enabling programmatic RPC discovery
- Stronger mempool policy — improved transaction handling
- DDoS fix — patched vulnerability in proof verifiers

### Zaino 0.2.0-rc.5

ZingoLabs' Zcash indexer [released rc.5](https://github.com/zingolabs/zaino/releases/tag/0.2.0-rc.5) — now supports Zebra 4, fixes zero-value shielded transaction database writes, and adds time-based sync logging to prevent stalls.

### Shielded Voting PRs Opened

Developer Greg Nagy [opened PRs](https://x.com/i/status/2035103640130625697) to implement shielded voting directly in Zodl repos. Privacy-preserving on-chain governance — vote without revealing direction or weight. ZIPs forthcoming. Retweeted by Sean Bowe (@ebfull).

### Engineering Office Hours #4: Memo Bundles

## Privacy Corner: Why Amount Matters

**The shielded pool is an anonymity set.**

When you shield ZEC, you join a crowd of other shielded transactions. The larger that crowd, the harder it is to link your deposit to any withdrawal.

But here's the catch: if you shield an unusual amount like `3.7142 ZEC`, you might be the only transaction with that exact value. You're in the anonymity set — alone.

**Better approach:** Shield round amounts (1, 5, 10 ZEC). These common values have hundreds of matching transactions. More matches = stronger privacy.

**Best approach:** Don't move funds unless you need to. Every transaction is a data point. The strongest privacy comes from keeping your ZEC shielded and still — zodl.
