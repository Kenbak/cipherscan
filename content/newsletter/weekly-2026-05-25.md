## Top Stories

### ZIP 2005 Merged: Quantum Recoverability Is Real

Sean Bowe announced that ZIP 2005 — Zcash Quantum Recoverability — has been [merged as Proposed](https://x.com/ebfull/status/2056841715127062664). This isn't a research paper or a roadmap item. It's merged code headed toward wallet integration.

The mechanism: as wallets update to support ZIP 2005, user funds automatically migrate to quantum-recoverable addresses. No network upgrade required for this step. No user action beyond updating their wallet.

Why it matters now: Google published a paper suggesting Q-Day could arrive by late 2029. Zcash is the only major cryptocurrency with a concrete, shipping response.

### NU7-rc0 Testnet Goes Live

The Valar Group [launched the first NU7 release candidate testnet](https://forum.zcashcommunity.com/t/55783). What's running on it:

- **25-second block times** (down from 75s — 3× faster confirmations)
- **Doubled Orchard TPS** capacity
- **Action Limits** to reduce sandblasting attacks
- **Network Sustainability Mechanism** for long-term miner incentives

This is the first opportunity for wallets and applications to test upcoming protocol changes against live infrastructure. The testnet is ephemeral (two weeks), with a more polished version coming after. Anyone can join in observer or mining mode — [instructions and public nodes here](https://nu7.valargroup.dev/).

### ZEC on Bitstamp by Robinhood

Bitstamp [announced ZEC availability](https://x.com/Bitstamp) through its Robinhood integration. Another access point in an expanding distribution network — Coinbase, Robinhood, Gemini, Kraken, and now Bitstamp all carry ZEC. Distribution is the compounding variable that most people underestimate.

## Privacy Corner: The Surveillance Ratchet

*Inspired by [Mastering Zcash](https://maxdesalle.com/mastering-zcash/) by Maxime Desalle — Chapter 5: "The Philosophy of Privacy"*

Surveillance capabilities only move in one direction: expansion.

Governments accumulate data, build systems, hire analysts, and develop analysis techniques. They share information across agencies and across borders. The infrastructure of surveillance, once built, does not get dismantled — it gets upgraded.

AI is accelerating this dramatically. Pattern matching that once required teams of analysts can now be automated. Metadata that once sat in silos can now be correlated at scale. Behavioral analysis that once took months can happen in real time. The cost of surveillance per person drops toward zero.

On transparent blockchains, the data is everything. Every transaction you've ever made is permanently preserved, waiting for better analysis tools. The blockchain does not forget, and neither do the adversaries mining it for information.

**What you do today will be analyzed with the tools of tomorrow.** Transactions that seem anonymous now may be trivially traceable in five years. Patterns hidden in noise today may be obvious signals once the algorithms improve.

This is why Zcash encrypts transactions rather than obfuscating them. Encryption doesn't degrade over time. You cannot find patterns in data that does not exist.

The question isn't whether surveillance tools will improve. They will. The question is whether the data will be there when they do.

Shield your ZEC. The ratchet only turns one way.
