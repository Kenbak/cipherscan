## Top Stories

### Two Critical Zebra Releases in 48 Hours

Zcash Foundation released Zebra 4.5.0 on May 29, patching 12 security vulnerabilities in a single release — including a consensus-critical P2SH sigop undercount (GHSA-gf9r-m956-97qx, reported by @samsulselfut). That fix itself contained a regression, prompting an immediate 4.5.1 hotfix the next day (GHSA-2prc-cj5x-4443, caught by @sangsoo-osec). 4.5.0 also added shielded coinbase support (ZIP-213) — miners can now receive block rewards directly to shielded addresses. All nodes must be on 4.5.1. [4.5.0 thread.](https://forum.zcashcommunity.com/t/55912) · [4.5.1 thread.](https://forum.zcashcommunity.com/t/55920)

### Three Wallets Ship Major Features in One Week

Noir went public on Chrome Web Store and shipped ZECFi (one-click ZEC collateral, in-wallet swaps via RHEA/NEAR Intents). Vizor expanded to Windows and Linux with native swaps. Zwap launched trustless shielded cross-chain swaps — no MPC, no multisig, no custodian. The Zcash wallet space hasn't been this competitive since launch. [Noir ZECFi.](https://x.com/noir_wallet/status/2060017175000449332) · [Vizor.](https://vizorwallet.com) · [Zwap.](https://zwap.app)

### ZCG Shuts Down Bug Bounties — AI Slop Killed the Program

ZCG terminated its Security & Vulnerability Disclosure Initiative on May 26 after AI-generated speculative reports overwhelmed the review pipeline. They cited curl's identical experience — Daniel Stenberg called it "death by a thousand slops." ZCG is now working with **Zellic** on access to **V12**, an AI-assisted auditing platform for proactive, repeatable review. **Least Authority** remains ecosystem security lead. Responsible disclosure stays open — but bounties are done. [Forum.](https://forum.zcashcommunity.com/t/55855)

## Privacy Corner: Confidentiality Is Not Privacy

This week Circle froze over $12 million in USDC sitting inside Zama's smart contract. One phone call, one blacklist entry, funds gone. The users thought they had "confidential" transactions. They did — right up until someone with a key decided otherwise.

This is the distinction people keep missing: **confidentiality** means someone *chose* not to look. **Privacy** means they *can't*.

A confidential stablecoin on a permissioned ledger is a curtain. Anyone with authority can pull it open. The issuer can freeze you. The chain operator can censor you. The regulator can compel disclosure. Your "privacy" exists at the pleasure of a counterparty.

Zcash doesn't work that way. There is no issuer to call. No admin key to revoke. No blacklist to add you to. The cryptography is the guarantee — not a policy, not a terms-of-service clause, not a pinky promise from a compliance department.

Eric Hughes wrote it in 1993: *"Privacy is the power to selectively reveal oneself to the world."* The operative word is *power*. If someone else holds the off switch, you don't have privacy. You have a favor.

When you hold shielded ZEC, the privacy is yours. Not delegated. Not revocable. Yours.
