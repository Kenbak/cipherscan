## Top Stories

### Zebra 5.2.0 Hardens Against Chain Splits

The Zcash Foundation shipped Zebra 5.2.0, expanding the local rollback window from 99 to 1,000 blocks — a direct response to the NU6.2 chain-split incident in early June. The old 99-block window covers roughly 99 minutes at Zcash's block pace. The new 1,000-block window provides coverage for reorgs of up to ~20 hours, giving operators substantially more time to respond to a sustained consensus split. All nodes running 5.1.1 or earlier will reach end-of-support around July 25. [Forum #56279](https://forum.zcashcommunity.com/t/zebra-5-2-0-wider-rollback-window/56279) · [Release](https://github.com/ZcashFoundation/zebra/releases/tag/v5.2.0)

### Zaino 0.4.0 Stable — Then 0.5.0 Pre-Release

Zaino's first stable release (0.4.0) shipped June 18, adding batch block writes, a short-sync accumulator path, NU6.2 compatibility, and RPC restricted to private addresses by default. Three days later, 0.5.0-rc.3 dropped — removing the legacy finalized-state schema and gating `gettxoutsetinfo` behind a feature flag. The pace signals urgency around the Ironwood migration path. Anyone on 0.4.x should stay there for production; the 0.5.0 line is a breaking pre-release. [Zaino 0.4.0](https://github.com/zingolabs/zaino/releases/tag/0.4.0) · [Zaino 0.5.0-rc.3](https://github.com/zingolabs/zaino/releases/tag/0.5.0-rc.3)

## Privacy Corner: When Your Phone Number Becomes Your Identity

Mexico's new regulation (effective June 30) requires linking every SIM and eSIM to a government-issued ID. Miss the deadline, your line gets suspended.

On the surface, this is about reducing crime. In practice, it collapses the boundary between communication and identity. Once your phone number is tied to your passport, every app, every payment, every service that authenticates via SMS inherits that link. A Zcash transaction sent from a phone whose number is tied to your identity isn't private by default — it's private only if the layers between you and the chain are airtight.

This is why protocol-level privacy matters more than app-level privacy. If the phone knows who you are, the app knows who you are, and the only thing standing between that identity and your financial activity is whether the chain itself leaks information. Transparent blockchains fail this test completely. Zcash passes it — but only if you're using shielded transactions with a wallet that doesn't phone home.

Mexico isn't unique. Turkey, India, Pakistan, Nigeria — over 150 countries now mandate SIM registration. Each one narrows the set of people who can transact privately without protocol-level encryption. The demand for Zcash doesn't come from ideology. It comes from policy.
