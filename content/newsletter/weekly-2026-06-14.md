## Top Stories

### Anthropic Audits Zcash Protocol — No Serious Bugs Found

Two weeks after Taylor Hornby's discovery of the Orchard counterfeiting vulnerability, Shielded Labs worked with Anthropic to review the Zcash protocol using Mythos, with prompts provided by Taylor (@earthrise). No serious vulnerabilities identified. More details on the review and findings coming in a future post. The protocol is now on firmer ground than it was before the bug was found. [Zooko's tweet.](https://x.com/i/status/2065541979979784341) [[Forum #56183](https://forum.zcashcommunity.com/t/56183)]

### Ironwood Specification Finalized

ZODL, working alongside Tachyon, Valar Group, ZF, and Shielded Labs, finalized consensus rule changes for Ironwood. The new pool uses the existing Orchard protocol with the corrected circuit, disables Orchard bundles in coinbases, and implements a turnstile mechanism for supply verification. Old pool freezes to new outputs; existing funds exit through the turnstile. Activation targeted for late July, following zcashd end-of-support. [[Forum #56044](https://forum.zcashcommunity.com/t/ironwood-verifying-the-soundness-of-zcash-s-circulating-supply/56044)]

### Zebra v5.1.1: Upgrade or Get Shut Down

ZF released Zebra v5.1.0 (June 11) and v5.1.1 (June 12) in rapid succession. The key change: end-of-support window reduced from 105 to 44 days. With NU7 expected in late July, any node still running older versions will auto-shutdown before activation. This is a forcing function — upgrade now. [[GitHub v5.1.1](https://github.com/ZcashFoundation/zebra/releases/tag/v5.1.1)] [[Forum #56152](https://forum.zcashcommunity.com/t/zebra-5-1-0-release/56152)]

## Privacy Corner: The Cost of Migration

Ironwood will ask every Orchard user to migrate their funds from the old pool to the new one. This migration is necessary — it's how the network proves the integrity of its supply. But it comes with a privacy tradeoff worth understanding.

When you move funds through the turnstile, two things become visible: the amount transferred and the approximate timing. An observer can see that X ZEC left the old Orchard pool and Y ZEC entered the new one within a certain window. If you're the only person migrating at 3am on a Tuesday, the link between your old and new pool activity becomes trivially obvious.

This is the cost of provable supply integrity. The protocol can either hide amounts perfectly forever (at the cost of undetectable counterfeiting risk) or it can periodically verify that no counterfeit coins exist (at the cost of revealing migration amounts). Ironwood chooses the latter — and it's the right choice.

What you can do: migrate when others are migrating. Don't rush. If your wallet supports batched or delayed migration, use it. The larger the crowd moving at the same time, the smaller your individual signal. This is the same principle that makes the shielded pool work in the first place — your privacy is proportional to the crowd you blend into.
