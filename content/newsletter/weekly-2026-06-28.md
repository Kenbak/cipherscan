## Top Stories

### ZCG Clears the Backlog — $455K Feature Branch Testnet Approved

The June 22 ZCG meeting was the most consequential grant session in months. Approved: **Feature Branch Testnet** ($455K — dedicated testnet for NU7-deferred features like ZSAs and NSM), **Batch-vs-Single Verification Fuzzing**, **Zaino Release Stabilization**, **Zcash Ghana**, and **Zcash Türkiye 2026**. Declined in the same session: Cross-Implementation ZIP Conformance, Zcash Studio, Zallet Operability (Z3 stack), Z-Vault, ZPAD-20, Hostile-Companion Fixture Pack, Zcash Arabia, and Zinfra Africa. The Feature Branch Testnet is notable — it gives ZSA and NSM developers a sandbox that runs in parallel with mainnet, unblocking work that was stalled by the Ironwood pivot. [Meeting Minutes](https://forum.zcashcommunity.com/t/zcash-community-grants-meeting-minutes-6-22-2026/56384)

### ZODL Joins the Blockchain Association

ZODL officially joined the Blockchain Association, the largest US-based crypto industry trade group. This is a DC policy play — putting Zcash's privacy-first position directly in front of the lobbying apparatus that shapes US digital asset regulation. Cypherpunk called it "a win for Zcash." Combined with PGPZ's June 30 inaugural breakfast and the DC Privacy Summit in planning, there's now an organized Zcash presence in Washington for the first time. [Tweet](https://x.com/i/status/2069089564451352756)

### Ironwood ZIPs Take Shape — Fees, Migration, and Deployment Spec

Three critical PRs landed in zcash/zips this week: **ZIP 317** adds Ironwood-pool action fees via a revisions mechanism (PR #1314, June 27), **ZIP 256** specifies deployment of consensus bug fixes between NU6.1 and NU6.2 (PR #1295), and the draft **Ironwood migration ZIP** (PR #1299) continues to evolve. Additionally, protocol spec refactoring for Ironwood (PR #1300) is actively being worked. The migration ZIP now specifies a privacy-preserving procedure for moving Orchard funds to Ironwood — but explicitly notes it does not yet account for proposed anchor height/auth data changes. Wallet devs should track this carefully. [ZIP 317 PR](https://github.com/zcash/zips/pull/1314) · [Migration PR](https://github.com/zcash/zips/pull/1299)

## Protocol & Development

### Zaino 0.4.2-rc.1 — Prometheus Metrics

Released June 26. Adds comprehensive metrics (sync lifecycle, gRPC, outbound RPC, mempool), a Prometheus `/metrics` endpoint behind a feature flag, and structured tracing fields across all crates. This is infrastructure observability — node operators can now wire Zaino into Grafana dashboards. [Release](https://github.com/zingolabs/zaino/releases/tag/0.4.2-rc.1)

### Ironwood Implementation Pace

The zcash/zips repo saw 9+ commits on June 24 alone — almost exclusively Ironwood/NU6.3 spec cleanup, ZIP 258 (consensus branch ID for the deployment), and rendering pipeline improvements. Sean Bowe RT'd Lean formal verification research from the Ethereum Foundation, signaling cross-pollination on circuit verification tooling. Activation remains targeted for late July at block height 3,417,100 (following zcashd end-of-support).

### US Pulls PQC Migration Deadline to 2031

Executive order signed moving the US post-quantum cryptography deadline from 2035 to 2031. Zcash's Ironwood includes quantum-recoverable wallets. Mert (Helius CEO): "The new pool will be quantum recoverable, with full quantum-proofing within the year."

## Privacy Corner: When AI Has God Mode

Cypherpunk's tweet this week wasn't hyperbole. Governments are already deploying AI to correlate financial data across systems. What changes with frontier models isn't the *intent* to surveil — that's always existed — it's the *cost*.

Before AI: cross-referencing financial records across institutions required human analysts, subpoenas, and time. The friction was the privacy. An investigator could surveil a target, but not everyone. The economics made mass surveillance of financial behavior impractical.

After AI: a model can ingest every transparent blockchain transaction, every KYC record, every exchange deposit, every timing correlation — and produce a probabilistic identity graph for every address on the network. The cost drops to pennies per query. What was once targeted becomes universal. The friction is gone.

This is why "I have nothing to hide" fails as an argument. You might have nothing to hide from a human analyst with limited time. You absolutely have things to hide from an AI that can correlate your coffee purchase timing with your commute pattern with your salary deposit schedule — and sell that profile to your insurance company.

Protocol-level encryption is the only countermeasure that scales against AI-powered surveillance. Zcash encrypts the transaction graph at the base layer. No amount of AI can deanonymize what was never exposed.
