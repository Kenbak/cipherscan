## Top Stories
### Grayscale Files World's First Privacy Coin Spot ETF

Grayscale Investments submitted Form S-3 with the SEC to convert its $200M+ Zcash Trust into a physically-backed spot ETF, trading on NYSE Arca as $ZCSH. If approved, it would be the first US spot ETF for a privacy-focused cryptocurrency. Coinbase Custody serves as custodian and Bank of New York Mellon as administrator. The filing came just 48 hours after the CLARITY Act cleared the Senate Banking Committee 15–9, which provides regulatory clarity for digital commodities — a framework that benefits ZEC's positioning. Grayscale has successfully converted 10 crypto trusts to ETFs since early 2024 with a 100% success rate. The trust currently trades at roughly a 7% discount to NAV, a gap an ETF structure would close. [CryptoBriefing](https://cryptobriefing.com/grayscale-zcash-spot-etf-filing/)

### NU7 Coinholder Polling Launches — First In-Wallet Governance Vote

Josh Swihart posted the formal NU7 sentiment poll with 6 key questions covering NSM issuance smoothing, fee reissuance timing, Sprout deprecation, memo bundles (ZIP-231), faster block times (75s→25s via ZIP-218), and the overall NU7 schedule. Community feedback is open until May 25, with the actual vote running June 10–24.

For the first time, Zcash coinholders will vote directly inside the Zodl mobile wallet — 1 ZEC = 1 vote, with MPC-encrypted ballots and publicly auditable results. ValarDragon's team built the privacy-preserving voting mechanism using PIR snapshots. Keystone hardware wallet users are also supported. This is the most significant governance milestone for Zcash in 2026. ## Privacy Corner: Why In-Wallet Governance Matters for Privacy
Zcash's new coinholder polling mechanism doesn't just give users a voice — it does so privately. Here's why that matters.

Traditional crypto governance (Snapshot, Tally, etc.) links your wallet address to your vote. Anyone watching the chain can see which addresses voted which way, how much weight they carry, and potentially correlate votes to real-world identities.

Zcash's approach is different. The Zodl polling system uses **PIR (Private Information Retrieval)** to take balance snapshots without revealing which addresses hold how much. Votes are **MPC-encrypted** — no single party (including ZODL) can see how individuals voted until results are published in aggregate.

**Practical tip:** When participating in the upcoming NU7 vote, you don't need to move your ZEC or expose your balance. Simply vote from within Zodl — your shielded balance is your ballot, and your preferences stay private. This is what governance looks like when privacy is the default, not a bolt-on.
